Hi, I am Diva, a Ph.D. graduate from Drexel University's College of Computing and Informatics. My research areas include Human-Computer Interaction, Computer-Supported Cooperative Work, and Technology for Underrepresented Populations. Specifically, I use qualitative and mixed-methods research approaches to design emerging technologies for family caregivers in the contexts of parenting and dementia caregiving.

I currently work as a UX Researcher at EdPlus at Arizona State University wherein I use qualitative and mixed-methods research approaches to make education accessible to everyone. Previously, I worked as a UX Researcher at Vanguard, where I led an end-to-end mixed-methods discovery study and an unmoderated remote usability study. In Summer 2022, I worked at Amazon as a UX Research intern to conduct a qualitative DEI evaluation using contextual inquiry. I completed my Master of Science in Human-Computer Interaction from University of Maryland, College Park, in 2019, and my Bachelor in Design from Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, in 2015.

Motivational Interviewing Conversational Agent Aiding in Adoption of Healthy Eating Habits in Parents of Young Children

Obesity is one of the leading causes of chronic illnesses in American children today. Parents’ feeding styles and preferences towards food plays a significant role in children's eating patterns. However, parents often face additional burdens such lack of time and motivation that hinder them from making healthy food choices for their children. Motivational Interviewing (MI) technique is a proven effective counseling method that helps individuals to discover motivations and strategies for personalized behavior change, especially for diet modification. We developed a conversational agent on Raspberry Pi using a MI conversation script with the help of an MI expert. We conducted studies with parents of young children who wanted to eat healthier together with their children. To test the perceptions and effectiveness of the developed prototype, we conducted studies including pre- and post-test questionnaires together with interviews.

Methods Used: User testing, Pre- and Post-test Questionnnaires, Semi-structured interviews, Descriptive and Inferential Statistics, Thematic Analysis

Related Publications: JMIR Human Factors, PervasiveHealth '20, CHI '20 workshop

Privacy Control in Conversational Agent Development

The CHI community increasingly develops and evaluates conversational agents for various areas such as health, business, or education. Conversational agents have become widely integrated with third-party applications. This integration allows these third-party platforms to collect end-users’ personal information so that conversational agents can provide personalized marketing and experiences to the end-users. It is unclear, however, which third-party applications are integrated with various conversational agent development platforms and how explicit their involvement is to the end-users. We address this knowledge gap by first examining conversational agent development platforms and the information given at the set-up process regarding various parties’ access to end-users’ information.

Methods Used: Chatbot platform selection, Simulation of chatbot development process, Thematic Analysis

Related Publications: AMIA'21, CHI '21 workshop

"Bond" Application


Video : Defines the Problem and the proposed Solution


  • Tags: User Experience, UX Design, User Research, Prototyping
  • Date: Sep 2017 - Nov 2017
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Sketch, Invision, Adobe Premiere Pro, JavaScript, HTML/CSS

The Problem :

DIGITAL COMMUNICATION FOR LONG DISTANCE RELATIONSHIPS (FRIENDS, FAMILY OR ROMANTIC) IS NOT IMMERSIVE.

What our target audience meant by NOT IMMSERSIVE?

  • - Lack of context while communicating through technology
  • - Nuances of conversation (non-verbal cues) get missed out
  • - Too much effort required to keep the conversation going
  • - Fear of missing out on events occuring in other person's life

Our Solution :

The "BOND" application which captures the Mood, Time, Weather, Battery Level, Messages, Pictures and Videos of the users effortlessly in a chronological order, to be displayed on the loved one's phone, or an Ambient Display.

Takeaways :

My role in this project was in the Research and Ideation phase, especially with the problem statement formation, wireframing, prototyping and video prototyping. Features from various different sketches got incoporated into the final designs. From working with a diverse team, I got to understand more about people from different cultures. Boarding Pass Re-design



Using the Gestalt Priciples, an old, cluttered boarding pass had to be redesigned to make more sense of the information.

  • Tags: Information Architecture, Visual Design, Gestalt Principles
  • Course: Fundamentals of HCI, UMD College Park
  • Date: Feb 2018
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Sketch, Pen & Paper

Image : Old, boring, cluttered boarding pass


Image : New, redesigned by me


Using the Gestalt Principles, information is categorised as:

  • Vertically : The details needed to find your flight and seat are in the leftmost, the details required to get into the flight are at the center, and the details required for later part of the flight are at the rightmost.

  • Horizontally : First row includes details required when arriving at an airport, and the Second row gives details for entering the flight and landing.

  • The important and similar information are grouped togther in the Blue box, keeping in mind the Law of Similarity.

  • A visual aid showing the postion of the seat in the flight is also provided, since attention span of users is less, and a lot of confusion is generated while finding seats.

  • A QR code is also added so as to make it easy for users to check flight statuses while in the airport, and reduce the load on the users' memory.
