Kidsteam @ HCIL, UMD


Image : Me leading a Kisteam session


Using the Cooperative Inquiry method of Participatory Design methodology, children are treated as design partners equal to adults to come up with design solutions for the proposed problem. As a Kidsteam Researcher in the Human Computer Interaction Lab at University of Maryland, College Park, my task is to facilitate equal participation among children and adults as designers, while at the same time retaining the child-like perspective to the design soluitons offered by the children.

  • Tags: Cooperative Inquiry, Participatory Design, Kidsteam, HCIL
  • Demographics of the Children: 8 - 13 years, Equal Male : Female ratio

Problem: DIGITAL COMMUNICATION FOR LONG DISTANCE RELATIONSHIPS (FRIENDS, FAMILY OR ROMANTIC) IS NOT IMMERSIVE

What do we mean when we say NOT IMMSERVIVE?

  • - Lack of context while communicating through technology
  • - Nuances of conversation (non-verbal cues) get missed out
  • - Too much effort required to keep the conversation going
  • - Fear of missing out on events occuring in other person's life
